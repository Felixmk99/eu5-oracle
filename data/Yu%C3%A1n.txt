Historical background
[
edit
|

After Genghis Khan united the Mongols in 1206, he and his successors launched several military campaigns southward into China between 1205 and 1279, culminating in the complete conquest and unification of China under Mongol rule. What first begun as a series of raids into the territory of the Western Xia in 1205-1208 eventually led to the complete conquest, subjugation and ultimately destruction of that state in 1209-1227. This was then followed by the Mongol conquest and destruction of the Great Jin in Northern China in 1211-1234, leading to the capture of Beijing in 1215. Lastly, the Mongol armies led by M√∂ngkhe Khan and later Kublai Khan struck south and conquered and destroyed the Song dynasty and the Dali kingdom in Central and Southern China in 1234-1279. With the defeat of the Song dynasty and with the desire to rule all of China, Kublai Khan officially proclaimed the establishment of the Yuan dynasty in 1279 and became Emperor of China, as Emperor Shizu of Yuan. The Mongols throughly looted, pillaged and ransacked China during their conquest and enslaved many Chinese, placing them at the bottom of the social hierarchy. The Imperial Keju Examinations were abolished and Han Chinese were expelled from the Imperial bureacracy, as the Mongoles entrenched their rule, with ordinary people experiencing prolonged and great hardship.
After Kublai Khan's death in 1294, the Mongol Empire fractured into four successor states; the
Yuan dynasty, the
Chagatai Khanate
,
Golden Horde
and the Ilkhanate as a civil war broke out. In 1304, a peace treaty was signed which established the Yuan dynasty as the nominal overlord of the other khanates, with the Yuan emperor as the Khagan of the whole Mongol Empire, but this had little effect in practice, as the four successor khanates continued to operate as separate states and ultimately fell separately as well. In the Yuan dynasty's case, this came in the form of military decline and factional strife within the court, coupled with a string of natural disasters and disease outbreaks in the 1330s and 1340s, which, when coupled with growing resentment by the Han Chinse against Mongol Rule, finally culminated in the outbreak of the Red Turban Rebellion, which eventually led to the collapse of the Yuan dynasty in 1368 and its succession by the
Ming dynasty
, founded in the same year by Zhu Yuanzhang, who became the Hongwu Emperor. The remnants of the Yuan government retreated north to Karakorum and continued to exist as a remnant state in Mongolia known as the Northern Yuan until 1635 when both it and the

were conquered by the Manchu-led Later Jin, which would go on to conquer China from the Ming and found the
